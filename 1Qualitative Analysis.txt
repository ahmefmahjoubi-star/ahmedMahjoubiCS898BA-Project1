1/Qualitative Analysis:

classical and optimization based algorithms handle complex outdoor textures and uneven lighting very differently as shown by the 3 separate segmentation results
in this particular outdoor setting Otsu's Global Thresholding was incredibly useless it just divides the image by extreme brightness (the sky and homes) and dark (the grass and figure) because it computes a single global threshold to distinguish foreground from background Its main drawback is that it completely combined the main figure with the shaded lawn, despite the fact that it was computationally straightforward because the figure bleed entirely into the ground there is no edge preservation of the target entity and a great deal of background noise
Adaptive Thresholding calculated thresholds locally in an effort to address the uneven illumination issue  this method is extremely sensitive to textures with high frequencies It read each every grass blade and background shadow as a distinct edge despite effectively avoiding Otsu's method's enormous light/dark split (a pro) as a result, there was a lot of background noise and static The primary figure's edge preservation was completely lost making it nearly impossible to discern it from the backdrop texture.
in the HSV color space K Means clustering produced the best segmentation K Means classified pixels according on color similarity rather than just pixel intensity  Its outstanding edge preservation the focal figure's silhouette is clearly isolated is its main advantage the primary drawback is the existence of slight background noise since the algorithm unavoidably grouped dark areas of the trees and porch structures that had the same color profile as the creature
Importantly the multi-channel color normalization carried out in Part 2 had a significant impact on this K Means result we achieved maximal mathematical contrast by implementing histogram equalization separately for each color channel this significantly increased the color space separation between the figure and its surroundings even if it produced visually odd "false colors" in comparison to the unprocessed dimly illuminated image from Homework 1 The raw image would not have the clear cluster boundaries required for KMeans to correctly extract the bio mass area without this intensive treatment
Otsu and Adaptive Thresholding: Due to the initial low-light, low-contrast conditions in the raw photos (Homework 1), these techniques would have failed even more severely probably producing masks that were entirely blank or solid color normalization of color served as a force multiplier the figures pixels were pushed into a different intensity range than the backdrop grass by independently equalizing the channels the figure was at least statistically detectable instead of being entirely lost in the original, muddy, low contrast raw image even though it didn't address the intrinsic flaws of global/adaptive thresholding.
