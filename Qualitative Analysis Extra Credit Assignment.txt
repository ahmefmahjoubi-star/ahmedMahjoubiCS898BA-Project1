The transition from the classical image processing techniques utilized in Homework 2 to the advanced Mask2Former deep learning approach yielded a drastic improvement in both quantitative metrics and visual accuracy. The primary challenge presented by the input image was its unconstrained, low-light environment. The scene suffers from poor illumination, high sensor grain, and a lack of stark edge boundaries. Most notably, the subject’s dark clothing shares a remarkably similar pixel intensity and color profile with the background elements, such as the shadowed grass, distant trees, and houses. Because classical methods rely almost exclusively on strict pixel value constraints and mathematical thresholds rather than contextual understanding, they struggled to properly isolate the subject from this complex background.

This limitation is highly evident when breaking down the specific failures of the Homework 2 algorithms. Otsu's Global Thresholding failed completely, producing an IoU of just 0.0303. Otsu’s algorithm relies on the assumption that the image has a bimodal histogram, attempting to find a single global threshold that minimizes intra-class variance. However, because the image is overwhelmingly dark, the algorithm incorrectly grouped the brighter sky and lighter patches of grass as the foreground, washing out the subject entirely in a block of white. Adaptive Thresholding, which scored an IoU of 0.0654, attempted to solve this by calculating thresholds for smaller, localized regions. Unfortunately, it reacted far too aggressively to the low-light sensor grain and subtle illumination gradients across the lawn. This resulted in severe, high-frequency "salt and pepper" noise across the entire image, as the algorithm hyper-focused on minor pixel variations rather than the overall subject. While K-Means Clustering performed the best among the classical methods with an IoU of 0.1546, it suffered from severe over-segmentation. K-Means clusters pixels strictly based on color and intensity values without any spatial awareness. Consequently, it could not semantically differentiate the dark pixels of the subject's clothing from the equally dark pixels of the background, mapping them to the exact same cluster.

In stark contrast, the advanced Mask2Former model successfully overcame the lighting and contrast limitations that completely derailed the classical algorithms. Instead of relying on raw pixel brightness or rigid mathematical clustering, the deep learning model utilized a transformer-based backbone to extract multi-scale features and establish spatial awareness. By leveraging semantic understanding, the model could identify the structural concept of a "figure" regardless of the localized pixel similarities with the background. The generated mask cleanly and precisely outlined the target subject, separating the figure from the surrounding dark grass and closely matching the complex posture defined in the hand-drawn ground truth mask.

Quantitatively, the advanced baseline method achieved an IoU of 0.7276 and a Dice Coefficient of 0.8424. This massive leap in performance underscores a fundamental principle of modern computer vision: while classical methods can act as a rudimentary baseline for highly controlled, well-lit scenarios, they lack the semantic robustness necessary for unconstrained, real-world data. The advanced deep learning method successfully ignored the background shadows, inherent camera grain, and overlapping color clusters, resulting in a highly accurate, noise-free segmentation.
